Conditions for doing emergency work

  1. (a) the activity is not a pre-planned emergency activity
  2. (b) the person carrying on the activity provides the regulator with notice in writing as soon as practicable of the carrying on of the activity and circumstances in which it was carried on
  3. (3) For the purposes of sub-paragraph (2)(a), a “pre-planned emergency activity” means any activity which has been planned in response to an emergency before it occurs.
  4. (4) The power of the regulator to serve a remediation notice under paragraph 9 of Part 1 of this Schedule applies where an activity has been carried on in reliance on this exclusion as if that activity were an unauthorised activity.

Conditions for building a footbridge

  1. you cannot carry out the activity on or within 500m of a site designated as a Special Protection Area (SPA), Special Area for Conservation (SAC), Ramsar site, Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI) or Local Nature Reserve (LNR). The locations of all these sites are available to view on MAGIC at www.magic.gov.uk
  2. you cannot carry out any work in within 100m of a river or lake in England or (if your activity is near the border) in Wales that is classified as of high morphological status under the Water Framework Directive. For information call the Environment Agency customer contact centre on 03708 506 506.
  3. you plan your works to ensure there are no significant adverse effects on protected or priority species of importance for nature conservation. You can find more information on the species that could be impacted by your works from your local record centre or Natural England's guidance on construction near protected areas and wildlife https://www.gov.uk/guidance/construction-near-protected-areas-and-wildlife
  4. the length of the footbridge measured from the top of one bank of the main river to the top of the other bank is no more than 8 metres
  5. the footbridge has no support in the watercourse, a deck width of no more than 1.5 metres and a kickerboard of no more than 100mm in height
  6. the footbridge does not reduce the cross-sectional area of the channel in the main river
  7. you cannot carry out the works within 100 metres of any non-agricultural building in the floodplain or another man-made structure on or in the main river
  8. the construction does not affect the bed of the main river
  9. the length of bank disturbed by the construction extends to no more than 1 metre on either side of the footbridge
  10. the footbridge is securely attached to foundations which are no closer than 1 metre to the edge of the bank
  11. construction of the footbridge does not require reinforcement of the bed or banks
  12. the approach ramp or steps for the footbridge do not extend more than 4 metres from the landward side of the bank
  13. the lowest point of the underside of the bridge is at least 600mm higher than the top of both banks of the main river
  14. you remove from the floodplain all excavated material not re-used on the site of the works
  15. the height of the land at each end of the footbridge is not changed by the construction
  16. any parapet of the footbridge is of open construction comprising (i)post and rail, (ii)post and wire mesh fencing of at least 100mm spacing; or (iii)post and wire strands.

See regulations

Conditions for building a outfall or headwall

  1. you cannot carry out the activity on or within 200m of a site designated as a Special Protection Area (SPA), Special Area for Conservation (SAC), Ramsar site, Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI) or Local Nature Reserve (LNR). The locations of all these sites are available to view on MAGIC at www.magic.gov.uk
  2. you cannot carry out any work in or within 100m of a river or lake in England or (if your work is near the border) in Wales that is classified as of high morphological status under the Water Framework Directive.   3. you cannot carry out any work in or within 200m upstream of a priority habitat that has been selected for the importance of its river or freshwater habitats. These are often called Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006 section 41 and section 42 habitats. For information call the Environment Agency customer contact centre on 03708 506 506.
  4. you plan your works to ensure there are no significant adverse effects on protected and priority species of importance for nature conservation. You can find more information on the species that could be impacted by your works from your local record centre (Link to LRC site) or Natural England's guidance on construction near protected areas and wildlife https://www.gov.uk/guidance/construction-near-protected-areas-and-wildlife
  5. the headwall is not located within 50 metres of another man-made structure on or in the main river
  6. if the headwall is on a non-tidal main river, the outfall pipe is aligned to an angle of between 30° and 60° to the direction of flow in the river
  7. the diameter of the outfall pipe is less than 300mm
  8. the height of the headwall is no more than 1.5 metres or no more than 75% of the height of the bank, whichever is less

  11. the headwall is not within 8 metres of a flood defence structure or river control works
  12. the outfall pipe does not pass through or under any culvert, remote defence, river control works or sea defence, or any raised embankment or wall forming part of the bank of the main river
  13. you remove from the floodplain all excavated material not re-used on the site of the works
  14. any pipe that discharges through the headwall does not pass within 8 metres of a flood defence structure.

Conditions for repairing bank slips and erosion using the fallen materials

  1. the works do not involve removal of material from the bed of the main river other than bank slippage;
  2. the works do not affect more than 10 metres of the bank at any one time;
  3. the works do not encroach into the channel of the main river beyond the line of the bank prior to the works being carried out;
  4. when the works are finished, the height of the bank does not exceed the lower of—
    • (i) the height of the bank on either side of the works, and
    • (ii) the height of the bank prior to the slip or erosion;
  5. the works are securely fastened to the bank at each end so as to prevent erosion behind the works;
  6. any repair of a bank slippage is made using as materials only material that has subsided from that bank;
  7. any repair of erosion uses materials of the same kind as those present on the relevant site;
  8. the works do not have a significant adverse effect on species included in a list published by the Secretary of State under section 41 of the Natural Environment and Rural Communities Act 2006, or by Welsh Ministers under section 42 of that Act(a), that are not protected species;
  9. the works are not carried out in, or within 100 metres of, a water body in England that is part of a main river classified as of high morphological status by the Agency in accordance with the relevant directions; and
  10. the works do not involve the use of a vehicle or of wheeled or tracked machinery on the bed or banks of the main river.
