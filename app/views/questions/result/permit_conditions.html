Conditions for doing dewatering

General rules

  1. The activity shall be completed withinn 6 months of the date on which the permit is granted
  2. The activities shall not extend beyond the land edged in green on the site plan
  3. The activities shall be undertaken outside of the relevant fish breeding season
  4. Make sure you're using sufficient competent persons and resources
  5. Make sure you keep records of your work

Design and scale of the work

  1. The activity shall affect no more than 20 metres of the bank of the watercourse at any one time
  2. The dewatering structure shall be in place for no more than 4 weeks
  3. The works are not allowed to happen within 8m of flood defences, or within 50m of other built structures on the river or within 100m of any non-agricultural building in the flood plain
  4. The depth of water adjacent to the dewatered area shall not exceed 1.2m
  5. The dewatering structure shall project into or over the main river no more than 10% of the width of the river
  6. The top level of the structure blocking and diverting the watercourse shall be lower than the ground level on the landward side at the foot of the bank, on each side of the main river.

Location of the work

The activities shall not be carried out within:

  1. 500m of a European designated nature conservation site, SSSI or National Nature Reserve
  2. 100m of an area identified as containing a Priority Species or Protected Species
  3. 100 metres upstream or downstream of a water body classified as having high morphological status
  4. 8 metres of a flood defence structure or river control work or drainage work
  5. 50 metres of a manmade structure in, over or under the watercourse
  6. 100 metres of any non-agricultural building in the floodplain

Keeping records of the work

  1. All records shall be retained for at least 6 years from the date when the records were made, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Environment Agency
  2. The operator shall send all reports and notifications required by the permit to the Environment Agency using the contact details supplied in writing by the Environment Agency
  3. Here is an example on how to keep records

Conditions for constructing an outfall pipe

General rules

  1. The activity shall be commenced within three years of the date of the grant of the permit and completed within 3 months of commencement
  2. The activities shall not extend beyond the land edged in green on the site plan
  3. The activities shall be undertaken outside of the relevant fish breeding season
  4. Make sure you're using sufficient competent persons and resources
  5. Make sure you keep records of your work

Design and scale of the work

  1. The diameter of the outfall pipe shall be 500 millimetres or less
  2. The width of the watercourse at the location where the outfall is to be installed shall be equal to or greater than 10 metres from bank top to bank top
  3. The total length of bank affected during construction of the outfall and headwall shall not be more than 3 metres
  4. The height of the headwall shall be no more than 75% of the height of the bank or 1.5 metres, whichever is less
  5. The gradient of the outfall pipe through the headwall should be less than 1:50 (fall in elevation:length)
  6. The outfall pipe shall be aligned to an angle of between 30° and 60° to the direction of flow in the watercourse
  7. The outfall pipe route shall not pass through or under any remote defence, river control work, sea defence, raised embankment or wall forming part of the bank of the main river
  8. The activity shall not be carried out within a culvert or bridge
  9. The outfall shall not be pumped
  10. The headwall, wing walls and apron shall not project beyond the existing line of the bank prior to the activity being carried out

Location of the work

The activities shall not be carried out within:

  1. 500m of a European designated nature conservation site, SSSI or National Nature Reserve
  2. 100m of an area identified as containing a Priority Species or Protected Species
  3. 100 metres upstream or downstream of a water body classified as having high morphological status
  4. 8 metres of a flood defence structure or river control work or drainage work
  5. 50 metres of a manmade structure in, over or under the watercourse
  6. 100 metres of any non-agricultural building in the floodplain

    7. Keeping records of the work

    1. All records shall be retained for at least 6 years from the date when the records were made, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Environment Agency
    2. The operator shall send all reports and notifications required by the permit to the Environment Agency using the contact details supplied in writing by the Environment Agency

    Conditions for installing a clear span bridge on a main river

    General rules

    1. The activity shall be commenced within three years of the date on which the permit is granted and completed within 3 months of commencement
    2. The activities shall not extend beyond the land edged in green on the site plan
    3. The activities shall be undertaken outside of the relevant fish breeding season
    4. Make sure you're using sufficient competent persons and resources
    5. Make sure you keep records of your work

    Design and scale of the work

    1. The bridge deck shall be no wider than 4.2 metres
    2. The soffit shall be at least 0.6 metres higher than the top of the bank on both sides of the river
    3. The watercourse shall be no wider than 8 metres measured from the bank top on one side to the bank top at the other side, at the location where the bridge is installed
    4. The bridge shall not be associated with, or lead onto or off, the public highway
    5. The approach ramp or steps for the bridge shall not extend further than is necessary to allow a gradient of 10% (1 metre vertical for 10 metres horizontal) on the approach
    6. The level of the land at each end and surrounding the approach ramp or steps to the bridge shall not be changed by the construction
    7. The bridge shall be securely attached to foundations which are no closer than a distance of 1 metre from the watercourse
    8. The length of the bank disturbed by the activity shall extend to no more than 2 metres to either side of the bridge
    9. Any parapet of the footbridge shall be of open construction comprising either post and rail, post and wire mesh fencing of at least 100mm spacing, or post and wire strands
    10. There shall be no light spill from artificial lighting into the watercourse

    Location of the work

    The activities shall not be carried out within:

    1. 500m of a European designated nature conservation site, SSSI or National Nature Reserve
    2. 100m of an area identified as containing a Priority Species or Protected Species
    3. 100 metres upstream or downstream of a water body classified as having high morphological status
    4. 8 metres of a flood defence structure or river control work or drainage work
    5. 50 metres of a manmade structure in, over or under the watercourse
    6. 100 metres of any non-agricultural building in the floodplain

          Conditions for installing a access culvert of no more than 5 metres length

          General rules

          1. The activity shall be commenced within 3 years of the date on which the permit is granted and completed within 3 months of commencement.
          2. The activities shall not extend beyond the site, being the land edged in green on the site plan attached to the permit
          3. The activities shall be undertaken outside of the relevant fish breeding season
          4. Make sure you're using sufficient competent persons and resources
          5. Make sure you keep records of your work

          Design and scale of the work

          1. The culvert shall be no longer than 5 metres in length
          2. The watercourse before the culvert is installed shall be no wider than 2 metres, measured from the foot of the bank in the channel on each side, over the full length of the culvert
          3. The culvert shall be on a straight reach of the watercourse and aligned with the direction of flow in the watercourse
          4. The slope of the culvert base shall match the slope of the bed of the watercourse
          5. The culvert shall have at least the same cross sectional area as the watercourse does before installation of the culvert
          6. The culvert shall be designed so that flood flows that exceed the capacity in the channel are able to bypass the structure and re-enter the watercourse downstream
          7. The capacity and design of the culvert shall ensure that it shall not cause increased flooding or erosion of the bank or bed, either downstream or upstream
          8. The base of the culvert shall be set below the existing bed, and clean natural gravel placed on top so that there shall be no step or drop in the final level of the bed
          9. The culvert shall not restrict the free passage of migratory fish and other fauna

          Location of the work

          The activities shall not be carried out within:

          1. 500m of a European designated nature conservation site, SSSI or National Nature Reserve
          2. 100m of an area identified as containing a Priority Species or Protected Species
          3. 100 metres upstream or downstream of a water body classified as having high morphological status
          4. 8 metres of a flood defence structure or river control work or drainage work
          5. 50 metres of a manmade structure in, over or under the watercourse
          6. 100 metres of any non-agricultural building in the floodplain

          Keeping records of the work

          1. All records shall be retained for at least 6 years from the date when the records were made, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the Environment Agency
          2. The operator shall send all reports and notifications required by the permit to the Environment Agency using the contact details supplied in writing by the Environment Agency

              Conditions for removing a total of 100 metres of exposed gravel from bars and shoals

              General rules

              1. The activity shall be commenced within three years of the date on which the permit is granted, and completed within 3 months of commencement
              2. The activities shall not extend beyond the site, being the land edged in green on the site plan attached to the permit
              3. The activities shall be undertaken outside of the relevant fish breeding season
              4. Make sure you're using sufficient competent persons and resources
              5. Make sure you keep records of your work

              Design and scale of the work

              1. Only dry gravels found above normal winter levels shall be removed
              2. The activity shall not be carried out upon the same part of a watercourse where any removal of gravel has been carried out within the previous 5 years
              3. The activity shall not be carried out where established vegetation is growing in or on the gravel, unless the vegetation consists solely of invasive non-native species
              4. The activity shall only be carried out in the dry, leaving a buffer of dry gravel of a minimum of 0.5 metre inside the wetted perimeter of the bar or shoal
              5. A minimum of one-third of the dry gravels shall be left undisturbed on the surface of each shoal at the upstream edge
              6. A maximum of 100 metres of exposed gravels shall be removed

                7. Location of the work

                The activities shall not be carried out within:

                1. 500m of a European designated nature conservation site, SSSI or National Nature Reserve
                2. 100m of an area identified as containing a Priority Species or Protected Species
                3. 100 metres upstream or downstream of a water body classified as having high morphological status
                4. 8 metres of a flood defence structure or river control work or drainage work
